Chronox

A digital health platform for managing chronic conditions.

Designed to help individuals living with Hypertension and Diabetes monitor, manage, and centralize their health data in one place.

The Problem & The Solution

The problem

Maxwel, a 46-year-old university professor living with hypertension, struggles to effectively manage his health despite using multiple tools. His health data is scattered across devices and apps, his tracking habits are inconsistent, and he often misses reminders. As a result, doctor visits feel unproductive, relying on incomplete information and guesswork. Like many others with chronic conditions, Maxwel isn’t lacking tools — he’s overwhelmed by disconnected systems that don’t work together.

The Solution

Chronox is a centralized health platform that helps people with chronic conditions manage their data, routines, and care in one place.

It enables users to:

  • Sync health data across devices
  • Get personalized reminders
  • Share accurate records with doctors

By simplifying fragmented tools into one system, Chronox helps users stay consistent and in control of their health.

Design process

Understanding the problem

We began by identifying the core issues faced by people living with chronic conditions like Hypertension and Diabetes. Many users struggled with managing fragmented health data across multiple devices and apps, which made health tracking inefficient and stressful.

User Research

We conducted a servey using Google Forms and received 45 responses: 19 respondents either had a chronic condition or had relatives who did. 10 of them specifically mentioned difficulties in monitoring their health data.

User Persona Development

To ensure we were designing with empathy and intention, we created detailed user personas based on real insights from our research i.e Maxwel — a busy professional living with Hypertension — to help us empathize with real user needs, behaviors, and pain points.

Wireframing

We sketched out the layout of the landing page using low-fidelity wireframes to define structure and information hierarchy. We also sketched out the Desktop App.This allowed us to quickly explore different layouts and ensure the flow of content was clear and goal-driven.

High-Fidelity Design

Using Figma, We created the high-fidelity design for the landing page, Deskstop app and also made it responsive , focusing on clarity, accessibility. We used soft, medical-friendly colors and a layout that speaks directly to the user’s needs.

Development

Once the high-fidelity landing page design was finalized in Figma, we ensured all components were organized and ready for a smooth handoff. The developers then implemented the designs and brought it to life. Throughout the process, we stayed available to answer any questions.

Low-Fidelity Design

We created low-fidelity wireframes to establish the landing page and desktop app’s core layout, navigation, and user flow. At this stage, the emphasis was on functionality and structure rather than visual design. This approach allowed for quick iterations and early feedback.

Reflection

The Chronox project brought together research, strategy, and design to create a connected health management experience for people living with chronic conditions. Over the course of the project, we designed five desktop screens — from the landing page to the dashboard and key feature interfaces — ensuring each one aligned with user needs for clarity, accessibility, and ease of use.

By centralizing health data, tracking medication, and enabling seamless communication with healthcare providers, Chronox offers more than just a set of features; it delivers peace of mind and empowers users to take control of their health with confidence.
